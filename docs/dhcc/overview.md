# Dubai Healthcare City (DHCC) — Overview

## What is DHCC?

Dubai Healthcare City (DHCC) is a government-backed healthcare free zone in Dubai, launched in 2002. It is governed by the Dubai Healthcare City Authority (DHCA) and spans two phases:

- **Phase 1 (Oud Metha):** Healthcare and medical education
- **Phase 2 (Al Jaddaf):** Wellness and expanded clinical facilities

## Scale and Reach

DHCC hosts over **10 hospitals**, **168+ clinical facilities**, and approximately **4,425 licensed healthcare professionals**. Recent growth figures cite **487 active facilities** and nearly **13,000 healthcare professionals** across the free zone.

DHCC serves both local residents and international medical tourists. In 2014, DHCC recorded 1.2 million patient visits, with 15% from medical tourists (GCC, Europe, Asia).

## Key Hospitals

Major JCI-accredited hospitals in DHCC include:

- Dr. Sulaiman Al Habib Hospital (Al Jaddaf, Phase 2)
- Mediclinic City Hospital (Oud Metha, Phase 1)
- Emirates Specialty Hospital (Oud Metha, Phase 1)
- Al Jalila Children's Specialty Hospital (Al Jaddaf, Phase 2)
- Moorfields Eye Hospital Dubai (Oud Metha, Phase 1)
- Zulekha Hospital Dubai
- American Academy of Cosmetic Surgery Hospital

## Patient Services

DHCC offers a **Find a Doctor / Service** portal (powered by Okadoc) where patients can:

- Search practitioners by specialty, clinic, language, gender, and insurance
- Book appointments online (confirmed in 25–45 seconds)
- Receive SMS/email reminders
- Cancel or reschedule 24/7
- Access telemedicine consultations across 70+ specialties

## Location and Access

- **Address:** Dubai Healthcare City, Dubai, UAE
- **Nearest airport:** Dubai International Airport (~10 minutes)
- **Metro:** Healthcare City Metro Station
- **Phases:** Oud Metha (Phase 1) and Al Jaddaf (Phase 2)

## Important Boundaries

DHCC reception cannot provide medical diagnoses or treatment advice. For emergencies, call **999** or visit the nearest emergency department.
