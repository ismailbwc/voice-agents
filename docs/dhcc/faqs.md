# DHCC Frequently Asked Questions

## Appointments

### How do I book an appointment at DHCC?

Use the Find a Doctor portal on dhcc.ae (Okadoc-powered), call the hospital directly, or ask the virtual receptionist to help find a doctor and guide you through booking.

### Can I cancel or reschedule online?

Yes. Appointments booked through the DHCC portal can be cancelled or rescheduled 24/7 via SMS/email notifications.

### How quickly is an appointment confirmed?

Online bookings are typically confirmed within 25–45 seconds.

## Insurance

### Does DHCC accept Daman insurance?

Yes. Most DHCC hospitals accept Daman and other major UAE insurers. Use the booking portal to filter doctors by insurance plan.

### Which hospitals accept international insurance?

Mediclinic City Hospital, Dr. Sulaiman Al Habib Hospital, Emirates Specialty Hospital, and Al Jalila Children's Hospital generally accept major international plans including Bupa Global and Cigna. Always confirm with the hospital billing office.

## Visiting DHCC

### What are DHCC visiting hours?

Hours vary by facility. Hospital emergency departments operate 24/7. Outpatient clinics typically operate 8:00 AM – 8:00 PM on weekdays. Check the specific clinic in our directory.

### Where do I park at DHCC?

Both Phase 1 (Oud Metha) and Phase 2 (Al Jaddaf) campuses have dedicated parking. Follow signage at each hospital entrance.

### How do I get to DHCC?

DHCC is accessible via the Healthcare City Metro Station, taxi, or car. Dubai International Airport is approximately 10 minutes away.

## Doctors and Specialties

### How do I find an English-speaking doctor?

Use the Find a Doctor portal or ask the receptionist to search by language. Many DHCC doctors speak English and Arabic, with additional languages available.

### Do I need a referral to see a specialist?

Many DHCC hospitals accept direct specialist appointments. Some procedures (surgery, advanced imaging) may require prior consultation or referral. Check with the specific hospital.

## Telemedicine

### Can I have a video consultation?

Yes. Many DHCC partners offer telehealth consultations. Okadoc supports virtual visits with fee payment online across 40+ specialties.

## Emergencies

### What do I do in a medical emergency?

Call **999** for ambulance service or go directly to the nearest hospital emergency department. DHCC hospitals have 24/7 emergency departments.

## Medical Records

Each DHCC provider maintains its own electronic health records. Request copies directly from the clinic or hospital where you were treated.
